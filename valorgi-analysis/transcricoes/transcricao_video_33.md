In the 1990s, Japan's new high-speed bullet trains ran into an unexpected problem.
As they entered tunnels at high speeds, they pushed the air forward, compressing it inside and forcing it out the other side with a loud boom that disturbed nearby residents.
One of the engineers who also happened to be a bird watcher noticed how Kingfisher's dive into water with barely a splash because of their long, streamlined beak.
So they redesigned the trains' nose to mimic the Kingfisher's shape eliminating the tunnel boom.
